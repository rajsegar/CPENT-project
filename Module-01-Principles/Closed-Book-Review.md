## Questions and corrected answers

### 1. What is penetration testing?

Penetration testing is an authorized simulated attack used to identify, validate, and prove real security impact.

### 2. What is the main difference between vulnerability scanning and penetration testing?

Vulnerability scanning uses automated tools to find known weaknesses. Penetration testing uses human analysis to validate exploitability, prove impact, collect evidence, and recommend remediation.

### 3. Why is written authorization required before testing?

Written authorization is required because it legally protects the tester, the client, and the systems being tested. It also defines scope, timing, rules of engagement, and testing limits.

### 4. What are the three main phases of penetration testing?

The three main phases are:

```text
Pre-Attack
Attack
Post-Attack
```

### 5. What happens during the Pre-Attack phase?

During the Pre-Attack phase, the tester prepares before exploitation. This includes planning, scoping, rules of engagement, reconnaissance, scanning, enumeration, and target understanding.

### 6. What happens during the Attack phase?

During the Attack phase, the tester safely validates weaknesses. This can include exploitation, gaining access, privilege escalation, lateral movement, and controlled post-exploitation.

### 7. What happens during the Post-Attack phase?

During the Post-Attack phase, the tester documents and restores. This includes evidence collection, cleanup, reporting, business impact explanation, remediation advice, and retesting if required.

### 8. What is the purpose of the EC-Council LPT methodology?

The EC-Council LPT methodology is a structured professional penetration testing process. It guides the tester from planning and scoping through reconnaissance, vulnerability assessment, exploitation, reporting, and remediation testing.

### 9. What is MITRE ATT&CK used for?

MITRE ATT&CK is used to map real attacker behaviour, including tactics, techniques, and procedures.

### 10. What is compliance-driven penetration testing?

Compliance-driven penetration testing is testing performed to meet legal, regulatory, contractual, or industry requirements such as PCI DSS, GDPR, HIPAA, SOC 2, and ISO/IEC 27001.
