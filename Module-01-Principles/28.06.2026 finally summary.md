## Key Takeaways

- Penetration testing is an authorized simulated attack.
- Vulnerability scanning finds possible weaknesses; penetration testing
  validates exploitability and impact.
- Written authorization, scope, and rules of engagement come before testing.
- The three main phases are Pre-Attack, Attack, and Post-Attack.
- Pre-Attack covers planning, reconnaissance, scanning, and enumeration.
- Attack covers controlled validation and exploitation within scope.
- Post-Attack covers evidence, cleanup, reporting, remediation, and retesting.
- The LPT methodology provides a structured professional testing process.
- MITRE ATT&CK maps adversary behaviour; it is not a penetration testing
  checklist.
- Artificial intelligence can assist a tester, but humans remain responsible
  for authorization, safety, verification, and reporting.

> No written authorization means no testing.
