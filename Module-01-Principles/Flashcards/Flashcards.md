# Module 01 — Flashcards

Q: What is penetration testing?
A: An authorized simulated attack used to test security controls and prove real-world risk.

Q: What is vulnerability scanning?
A: Automated identification of known vulnerabilities.

Q: What is the key difference between a scan and a pen test?
A: A scan finds issues. A pen test validates, exploits, chains, and explains impact.

Q: What separates a penetration tester from an attacker?
A: Written authorization, defined scope, legal permission, and professional intent.

Q: What are the three main phases?
A: Pre-Attack, Attack, Post-Attack.

Q: What happens in Pre-Attack?
A: Planning, scoping, reconnaissance, scanning, and enumeration.

Q: What happens in Attack?
A: Exploitation, privilege escalation, lateral movement, and controlled access.

Q: What happens in Post-Attack?
A: Cleanup, evidence collection, documentation, reporting, and remediation advice.

Q: What is lateral movement?
A: Moving from one compromised system to another inside the environment.

Q: What is business impact?
A: The real-world damage or risk a vulnerability could cause to the organization.

Q: What is PCI DSS for?
A: Payment card data.

Q: What is GDPR for?
A: EU personal data.

Q: What is ISO/IEC 27001 for?
A: Information Security Management System.

Q: Why is AI useful in penetration testing?
A: It can help with speed, automation, summarization, and scripting.

Q: Why does AI still need human oversight?
A: It can make mistakes, produce false positives, or miss context.
